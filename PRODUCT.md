# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Students in grades 6–9 and their teachers. Students arrive via an invite link to play story-driven grammar missions; teachers create, review, and publish rooms through a dashboard. Students should feel like they're playing a game, teachers should feel in control and confident in the tool.

## Product Purpose

Clause transforms grammar practice into themed escape-room adventures. Students solve grammar challenges across story stages (Sentence Surgery, Evidence Sort, Case File Rewrite), earn clue tokens, and unlock a final lock — all while being graded semantically by AI. Teachers can generate custom rooms with AI and monitor class performance.

What success looks like: students genuinely enjoy the experience and stay engaged; teachers trust the platform as both effective and professional.

## Positioning

Grammar practice that feels like an adventure and becomes a game of wits and discovery. Not a worksheet in a prettier wrapper — every mission is a story you solve with grammar.

## Brand Personality

Clever, Bold, Immersive. The tone is smart and playful without being childish — it respects the student's intelligence while making grammar genuinely exciting. Energetic but not frantic; dramatic but not dark.

## Anti-references

- Corporate / boring SaaS — gray cards, muted tones, generic dashboards
- Overly childish / cartoonish — feels too young for middle-schoolers
- Dark & edgy / horror-themed — too intense for the target age
- Too minimal / sterile — white space-heavy, feels empty and unfinished

## Design Principles

- **Thematic immersion first** — each game room is its own world with a distinct visual identity. The gameplay shell is consistent; the theme of each room transforms it.
- **Clever over cute** — interactions should feel smart and satisfying (discovery, unlock, reveal) rather than saccharine or condescending.
- **Bold but credible** — visually striking enough to captivate students while remaining professional enough for teachers to trust and adopt.
- **No AI defaults** — avoid the saturated patterns (warm cream backgrounds, card grids, section eyebrows, gradient text) that make AI-designed interfaces recognizable.
- **Every state matters** — loading, empty, error, and success states are designed, not afterthoughts. Feedback is clear and rewarding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: minimum 4.5:1 body text contrast, keyboard-navigable, screen-reader compatible. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color information is paired with text/icons, never used alone.
